# Code Of Conduct

## Our Standard

NAM-BOT is intended to be a friendly, respectful project.

Examples of behavior that help create a positive environment:

- Being respectful in disagreements
- Assuming good intent while still giving honest technical feedback
- Welcoming newer developers and people who are new to Electron, Python environments, or NAM workflows
- Focusing criticism on ideas, code, and behavior rather than people

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, threats, or abusive language
- Personal attacks or repeated bad-faith engagement
- Dismissing or mocking beginners for asking basic questions
- Publishing private information about another person without permission

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to project discussions, issues, pull requests, and other community spaces connected to NAM-BOT.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, or other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct.

## Reporting

If someone is behaving in a way that makes participation feel unsafe or hostile, contact the maintainer privately instead of escalating the conflict in public.
